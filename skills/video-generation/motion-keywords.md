# Motion Keywords

Vocabulary for describing movement in video generation prompts.

## Camera Movements

dolly-in/out (emotional proximity), pan (horizontal sweep), tilt (vertical sweep, power dynamics), track/lateral dolly (parallel motion), crane up/down (elevation change), steadicam (fluid following), handheld (urgency, documentary), static locked-off (stillness, tension), orbital (360-degree hero reveal), whip pan (percussive energy), push-in (tension building), rack focus (depth-based reveal), dolly zoom/vertigo (disorienting scale shift)

## Subject Verbs

Strong: walks, turns, grips, releases, leans, rises, flinches, exhales, settles
Force: slam, whip, recoil, drift, surge, snap, collapse, scatter
One dominant force per motion reads cleaner than multiple competing forces.

## Temporal Arc

Standard structure: setup (stillness) → action (primary movement) → settle (deceleration to held pose).
Accelerate into action, decelerate out. Hold the ending. Avoid cramming too many actions into 8 seconds.

## Physics Cues

momentum, weight transfer, inertia, settling, friction, bounce, gravity
Material behavior: hair sway after turn, cloth drape swing, liquid slosh, smoke disperse, glass shatter pattern

## Product-Safe Motion

Products should not use anthropomorphic verbs. "camera orbits product" not "product whirs to life." Avoid precise mechanical angles unless motorized parts are confirmed -- causes robotic animation artifacts.
