# Creative Direction

## Style Development Methodology

1. **Analyze the brief**: Identify tone, subject matter, target audience, and medium. What feeling should the viewer walk away with?
2. **Identify visual anchors**: Find 2-3 concrete visual elements that ground the direction -- a color palette, a lighting quality, a texture, a filmmaker's signature look.
3. **Propose 2-3 directions**, each with:
   - A short name (e.g., "Warm Nostalgia", "Clinical Precision")
   - Specific visual anchors (not abstract)
   - Reference touchpoints (films, photographers, art movements)
   - How it serves the brief's intent
4. **Refine iteratively**: Each round should be more specific -- moving from "warm tones" to "golden hour tungsten with fill from practical lamps, Kodak Vision3 250D warmth."

## Interpreting Creative Intent

Users express visual ideas in non-visual language. Translation patterns:
- "premium" -> high-contrast lighting, negative space, deliberate framing, slow camera movement
- "authentic" -> natural light, handheld micro-movement, practical locations, imperfect framing
- "pop" -> saturated color, high contrast, dynamic angles, bold composition
- "cinematic" -> anamorphic characteristics (oval bokeh, horizontal flares), 2.39:1 framing, motivated lighting, shallow DoF

Ask clarifying questions when intent is ambiguous. A single direction change early saves many regenerations later.

## Reference Image Interpretation

- White/neutral studio backgrounds: subject reference only, not style direction.
- Styled environments, mood boards: may indicate desired artistic direction.
- Studio product shots do not mean the user wants minimalist video aesthetic.
- Default to rich, detailed environments. Avoid bland minimalist studio settings unless requested.

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid AI cliche aesthetics: neon, minimalist, cyberpunk, brutalist defaults. Do not suggest these unless specifically relevant to the brand or user intent. Think like a creative director crafting a unique campaign for THIS specific brand, not filling a template. Avoid generic phrases: "stylish strategic commercial", "contemporary individual", "chic modern cityscapes."

## Proposing Directions

Draw from the project-style-keywords reference below. Styles can be blended, inverted, or extrapolated -- they are a starting point, not a constraint.

Cover five dimensions when composing direction:
1. **Visual surface**: color grade, lighting, texture, lens characteristics.
2. **Camera behavior**: movement patterns, framing discipline, angle preferences.
3. **Editorial rhythm**: cut pacing, transition style, montage structure.
4. **Emotional atmosphere**: mood, tension, energy, intimacy level.
5. **Narrative attitude**: tone of storytelling, relationship to subject, voice.
