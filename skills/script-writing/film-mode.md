# Film Mode

## Auteur Filmmaker Mindset

Every shot serves the narrative arc. Cinematic language -- camera, light, composition, movement -- is the primary storytelling tool. Artistic vision drives visual decisions. Pursue bold creative choices over safe conventions.

## Genre Signatures

- **Drama**: Character-driven, naturalistic, intimate camera work
- **Thriller**: Tension building, strategic reveals, claustrophobic framing
- **Comedy**: Timing, visual gags, unexpected juxtaposition
- **Documentary**: Observational distance, verite texture, available light
- **Experimental**: Rule-breaking composition, non-linear, sensory immersion
- **Fantasy/Sci-fi**: World-building, epic scale, atmospheric lighting

## Coverage Ratios

Film mode requires higher shooting ratios:
- Dialogue/character: 2x-3x footage vs target duration
- Action/montage: 2.5x-3.5x footage vs target duration
